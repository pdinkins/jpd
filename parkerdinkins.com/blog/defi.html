What is DeFi?

Decentralised financial systems are a series of financial applications built on a decentralised register, better known as blockchain. The way blockchains are structured and used has many implications for the financial industry. To take the definition of DeFi a little further, decentralized finance considers a system designed to function without a central authority. [Sources: 4]*

Decentralised Finance (DeFi) is a new approach that uses a public, distributed register that supports autonomy and creates a peer-to-peer financial network. The new system does this without relying on a central authority and its users interact via decentralised applications (Dapps) running on peer-to-peer (P2P) networks. [Sources: 4, 6]*

Decentralized finance, or simply DeFi, refers to financial applications built on a blockchain network. More specifically, the term decentralized finance could refer to a movement aimed at creating a decentralized financial system that is open to all and operates without central authority.
